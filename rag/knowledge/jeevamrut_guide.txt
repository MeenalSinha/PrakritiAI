JEEVAMRUT — THE FOUNDATION OF ZERO BUDGET NATURAL FARMING (ZBNF)

What is Jeevamrut?
Jeevamrut is a fermented liquid biofertilizer developed by Subhash Palekar as the cornerstone of Zero Budget Natural Farming. It activates the soil's native microbial ecosystem, dramatically improving soil fertility, structure, and plant immunity without any chemical inputs.

The name means "life nectar" (Jeeva = life, Amrut = nectar) in Sanskrit.

Standard Jeevamrut Recipe (200 Liters / 1 Acre Dose):
- 10 kg fresh desi cow dung (from indigenous breed Gir, Sahiwal, Red Sindhi — NOT Jersey/HF)
- 10 liters fresh desi cow urine (preferably morning collection)
- 2 kg jaggery (old, unrefined — not refined sugar)
- 2 kg gram flour / besan (any legume flour works)
- 1 handful of topsoil from the farm bund (contains native microbes)
- 200 liters clean water (non-chlorinated, preferably well or rainwater)

Preparation Method:
1. Fill a 200-liter drum with water
2. Add cow dung and dissolve completely (no lumps)
3. Add cow urine and stir
4. Add jaggery (feeds the microbes)
5. Add gram flour (protein for microbes)
6. Add the handful of topsoil
7. Stir clockwise for 5 minutes with a wooden stick
8. Cover with jute/gunny bag — NOT plastic (must breathe)
9. Place in shade — not direct sunlight
10. Stir twice daily (morning and evening) for 48 hours
11. After 48 hours of fermentation, Jeevamrut is ready to use

How to Use:
- Soil drench: Dilute 200L in irrigation water for 1 acre
- Foliar spray: Mix 10L Jeevamrut in 90L water — spray on leaves in morning
- Seed treatment: Soak seeds in Jeevamrut for 20 minutes before sowing
- Best time to apply: Early morning (5–8 AM) or evening after sunset

Application Frequency:
- First month: Every 15 days
- Thereafter: Monthly
- During flowering/fruiting: Every 10 days for maximum benefit

Why It Works (Science):
- Cow dung contains 300–500 crore beneficial bacteria per gram
- Jaggery provides carbon (feeds bacteria and fungi)
- Gram flour provides nitrogen and protein for microbe growth
- Native soil microbes get amplified 10,000x in 48 hours
- The fermentation product contains: nitrogen-fixing bacteria, phosphate-solubilizing bacteria, and growth-promoting Bacillus species

Expected Results:
- Visible improvement in soil tilth within 3 applications
- Crop color deepens (better chlorophyll production)
- Root development improves significantly
- Crop resistance to disease and drought increases
- Yield increases gradually over 3 seasons (avg. 15–30% by Year 3)

Storage:
- Use within 72 hours of preparation for maximum potency
- Can be stored 7 days in shade but potency reduces
- Never seal the container — CO2 from fermentation must escape

Troubleshooting:
- Bad smell (H2S): Too much dung or too little jaggery — add more jaggery and stir
- No fermentation after 48h: Check if water is chlorinated — use well water
- White mold on top: Harmless — stir it in
- Slippery feel: Perfect — indicates high microbial activity

Common Mistakes:
1. Using Jersey/HF cow dung — WRONG. Indigenous breed cow dung has 10x more bacteria
2. Using chlorinated tap water — kills the bacteria
3. Sealing the drum — prevents CO2 escape, fermentation fails
4. Applying in afternoon sun — UV kills microbes on leaves
5. Using fresh jaggery — old fermented jaggery works better
