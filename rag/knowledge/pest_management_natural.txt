NATURAL PEST MANAGEMENT — ORGANIC SOLUTIONS FOR COMMON CROP PESTS

PRINCIPLE: In Zero Budget Natural Farming, pest management is done ONLY using natural, farm-derived materials. Chemical pesticides destroy soil life and are strictly prohibited.

The 5-Layer Defense System in ZBNF:
1. Healthy soil → healthy plants → natural resistance
2. Biodiversity → natural predators control pests
3. Physical barriers (neem branches, sticky traps)
4. Botanical sprays (neem, tobacco, cow urine)
5. Jeevamrut to boost immunity (last resort intervention)

NEEM-BASED PREPARATIONS

Neem Oil Spray (Universal Pesticide):
- Mix: 5 ml neem oil + 1 ml liquid soap (emulsifier) + 1 liter water
- Shake vigorously until milky white emulsion forms
- Spray on leaves — both top and undersides
- Apply in evening to avoid phytotoxicity in sun
- Repeat every 7–10 days during pest pressure
- Effective against: Aphids, whiteflies, mealybugs, mites, thrips

Neem Leaf Extract (Neemastra):
- Ingredients: 5 kg fresh neem leaves + 5 liters cow urine + 1 liter water
- Boil neem leaves in water for 20 minutes
- Strain after cooling
- Add cow urine to the extract
- Dilute 1:10 with water for spraying
- Effective against: Caterpillars, leaf miners, beetles

Neem Seed Kernel Extract (NSKE 5%):
- Crush 500g neem seeds, soak in 10 liters water for 12 hours
- Strain and add 100ml cow urine
- This is NSKE 5% — spray directly without further dilution
- Most effective for sucking pests and borer control

TOBACCO DECOCTION (For Chewing and Sucking Pests)

Recipe:
- 1 kg dried tobacco leaves or 500g tobacco waste
- Boil in 10 liters water for 30 minutes
- Cool and strain
- Add 100g lime water (prevents nicotine breakdown in sun)
- Add 20 ml liquid soap
- Dilute 1:5 before use (final spray concentration)

Effective against: Aphids, jassids, thrips on cotton, groundnut, vegetables
WARNING: Do NOT spray on brassica vegetables — can cause leaf drop

COW URINE SPRAY (Direct Application)

Fresh cow urine can be diluted 1:10 with water and sprayed directly.
- Contains: uric acid, cresol, phenol — natural antiseptic
- Effective against: Fungal infections, soft-bodied insects
- Best results: Spray at dawn — microbes are activated by morning sun
- Weekly spray on fruit crops during monsoon prevents fungal spread

SPECIFIC PEST SOLUTIONS

Fall Armyworm (FAW) on Maize — Spodoptera frugiperda:
- Mix 5g/L Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) — spray into leaf whorl
- Alternatively: Push green chilli + garlic paste into whorl
- Pheromone trap: 5 traps per acre (replace lure every 3 weeks)
- Jeevamrut soil drench strengthens plant resistance

Aphids on Mustard, Wheat, Vegetables:
- Neem oil 5ml/L spray — highly effective
- Tobacco decoction diluted 1:5
- Yellow sticky traps: 6–8 per acre
- Encourage ladybug predators — plant flowering marigolds at border

Whitefly on Cotton, Tomato, Chilli:
- Yellow sticky traps (15–20/acre)
- Neem NSKE 5%
- Reduce nitrogen input — over-fertilized plants attract whiteflies

Stem Borer on Paddy and Maize:
- Trichogramma cards: 60,000 eggs per card, 5 cards/acre
- Apply when 1–2% stem shows deadheart symptom
- Coumaphos neem formulation into stem base

Diamondback Moth on Cabbage/Cauliflower:
- Bt spray (Bacillus thuringiensis) — 2.5g/L every 10 days
- Pheromone traps — 4 per acre
- Intercrop with garlic to repel adults

Mealybug on Cotton, Grapes:
- Neem oil 5ml/L + soap emulsifier
- Spray ethanol-washed with isopropyl on heavily infested spots
- Introduce Cryptolaemus montrouzieri predator beetles

Red Spider Mite on Chilies, Brinjal:
- Spray water forcefully on undersides of leaves (dislodges)
- Sulfur-based spray (wettable sulfur 3g/L) — acceptable in organic
- Neem oil effectively disrupts mite reproduction

MONITORING AND RECORD KEEPING

Weekly Pest Scouting Protocol:
1. Walk field in W pattern — check 20 plants at 5 spots
2. Record pest count and type
3. Check ETL (Economic Threshold Level) before spraying
4. Only spray if pest count exceeds ETL

ETL (Economic Threshold Levels) for Common Pests:
- Aphids on wheat: 20+ per tiller
- Aphids on mustard: 50+ per plant
- BPH on rice: 5+ per hill
- Bollworm on cotton: 5+ per 100 flowers

Source: ICAR-NCIPM Pest Management Guidelines, APCNF Pest Management Manual 2022, ZBNF Practice Handbook
