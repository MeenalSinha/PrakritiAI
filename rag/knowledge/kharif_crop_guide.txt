KHARIF SEASON CROPS — PLANTING GUIDE FOR NATURAL FARMING

KHARIF SEASON OVERVIEW:
Kharif crops are sown at the onset of monsoon (June–July) and harvested in October–November.
Primary states: All of India — especially Maharashtra, Punjab, Haryana, UP, MP, Bihar, Karnataka

KEY KHARIF CROPS AND NATURAL FARMING CALENDAR:

PADDY (RICE) — Oryza sativa:
Varieties for Natural Farming:
- Traditional: Sona Masuri, Kolam, Govind Bhog, Kalanamak, Lalri, Navara (medicinal)
- Avoid: Hybrid varieties — require more inputs, not suitable for NF

Planting Calendar:
- Nursery sowing: 15 June – 30 June
- Transplanting: 15 July – 15 August (Kharif)
- Harvest: October – November

Seed Rate: 25–30 kg/acre (transplanting); 40–50 kg/acre (direct seeding)
Spacing: 20cm x 20cm (SRI method) or 25cm x 25cm

SRI Method (System of Rice Intensification) for NF:
1. Transplant 12–14 day seedlings (not older — very important)
2. Single seedling per hill — NOT bunches
3. Spacing: 25cm x 25cm
4. Moist soil — NOT continuous flooding (saves 30% water)
5. Apply Jeevamrut at 15, 30, and 45 DAS
6. Weed with rotary weeder — aerates soil, improves yield

Water Management in Paddy:
- NF paddy needs 30–40% LESS water than conventional
- Use "Wet-Dry" cycles: flood for 2 days, drain for 2 days
- Do NOT maintain continuous water logging — causes methane emission and root stress

Expected Yield with NF: 22–28 quintals/acre (vs 20–25 conventional)

COTTON (GOSSYPIUM HIRSUTUM) — Bt or Non-Bt:
Natural Farming Note: Non-Bt cotton varieties are recommended for NF systems
Varieties: YCSDH 32, Bunny (Non-Bt), Ankur, Narayani (indigenous varieties preferred)

Planting Calendar:
- Sowing: 1 May – 30 June (pre-monsoon or early kharif)
- Harvest: December – January
- Seed treatment with Beejamrut: Essential for good germination

Spacing: 60cm x 90cm (traditional); 120cm x 45cm (high density planting)
Seed Rate: 1.5–2 kg/acre

Jeevamrut Schedule for Cotton:
- Application 1: 15 DAS (soil drench 200L/acre)
- Application 2: 35 DAS
- Application 3: 55 DAS (boll formation stage — critical)
- Foliar spray: Weekly neem oil 5ml/L during boll weevil season

SOYBEAN — Glycine max:
Varieties for NF: JS 335, JS 93-05, MAUS 61, Phule Agrani
Planting: 15 June – 15 July | Harvest: October

Natural Advantage of Soybean:
- Nitrogen-fixing root nodules — adds 40–80 kg N/ha to soil
- After soybean, wheat needs 30% less jeevamrut application
- Intercrop soybean with maize (1:2 ratio) for best results

Jeevamrut Note: Soybean is nitrogen-fixer — reduce Jeevamrut nitrogen dose; focus on phosphorus (add rock phosphate)

MAIZE (MAKKA) — Zea mays:
Varieties: Ganga 5, Deccan 103, Shaktiman (open-pollinated preferred)
Planting: June–July | Harvest: October

Intercropping System (Palekar Method):
- Row 1: Maize
- Row 2: Arhar (pigeon pea)  
- Row 3: Cowpea (nitrogen fixer)
- Row 4: Maize
This 4-row pattern: Maize, Arhar, Cowpea, Maize provides wind protection, N-fixing, and maximum yield per acre

MOONG (GREEN GRAM) — Vigna radiata:
Varieties: PDM 54, SML 668, Pusa Vishal, MH 421
Duration: 60–70 days (very short crop — excellent for crop rotation)
Planting: June–July | Harvest: September

NF Advantage: Short duration, nitrogen fixer, leaves biodegradable mulch after harvest

GROUNDNUT (PEANUTS) — Arachis hypogaea:
NF Varieties: TG 38, JL 24, GG 7, Chaffa (indigenous varieties)
Planting: June–July | Harvest: October–November
Seed Rate: 60–70 kg/acre

Gypsum Application (Organic Source):
Apply 100 kg gypsum/acre at pegging stage — natural calcium source, prevents empty pods

CROP ROTATION RECOMMENDATIONS FOR KHARIF:

Year 1 Kharif → Year 1 Rabi:
Paddy/Wheat rotation (traditional, but nutrient-depleting)

BETTER ROTATION FOR NF:
Year 1 Kharif: Maize/Soybean intercrop
Year 1 Rabi: Wheat
Year 2 Kharif: Cotton or Paddy
Year 2 Rabi: Chickpea/Mustard
Year 3 Kharif: Vegetables (Tomato, Brinjal)
Year 3 Rabi: Potato/Onion

This rotation prevents disease buildup, maintains soil health, and reduces input needs.

SOIL TESTING BEFORE KHARIF SOWING:
Test for: pH, EC, N, P, K, Organic Carbon (OC), Zinc, Boron
Target values for most Kharif crops:
- pH: 6.5–7.5 (ideal)
- OC: >0.75% (increase with Jeevamrut over 3 seasons)
- N: 250–280 kg/ha (available)
- P: 22–25 kg/ha (available)
- K: 280–300 kg/ha (available)

Correction Using Natural Inputs:
- Low pH (acidic): Add lime (limestone powder) 200kg/acre
- High pH (alkaline): Add gypsum 100kg/acre
- Low phosphorus: Rock phosphate 50kg/acre
- Low zinc: Apply zinc solubilizing bacteria (ZSB) — available from KVK

Source: ICAR Kharif Crop Production Manual 2023, Subhash Palekar ZBNF Volume 3, TNAU Organic Farming Guide
