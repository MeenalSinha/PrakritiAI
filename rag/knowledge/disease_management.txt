CROP DISEASE MANAGEMENT — NATURAL AND ORGANIC TREATMENTS

PHILOSOPHY: In natural farming, plant diseases are symptoms of soil imbalance. The primary treatment is always soil restoration, not symptom suppression. Chemical fungicides are strictly prohibited.

UNDERSTANDING PLANT DISEASE CAUSES:
1. Soil deficiency (nutrient imbalance) — 60% of diseases
2. Wrong crop variety (susceptible to local diseases)
3. Overcrowding (poor air circulation)
4. Overwatering (fungal conditions)
5. Pest damage creating entry points for pathogens

FUNGAL DISEASES AND NATURAL TREATMENTS

Early Blight (Alternaria solani) — Tomato, Potato:
SYMPTOMS: Dark brown circular spots with yellow halo on older leaves. Concentric rings visible. Moves up from base of plant.

Natural Treatment:
1. Neem oil 5ml/L + soap 1ml/L — spray every 7 days
2. Cow urine spray (1:10 dilution) — apply at dawn
3. Copper sulfate 3g/L spray (allowed in organic — apply sparingly)
4. Remove and bury infected leaves immediately
5. Improve air circulation by removing lower leaves
6. Apply Jeevamrut soil drench to improve resistance
7. Mulch base of plants to prevent soil splash

Organic Preventive Spray (Panchgavya):
- Mix cow dung, cow urine, milk, curd, ghee
- Dilute 3% — spray as preventive measure weekly

Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans) — Potato, Tomato:
SYMPTOMS: Water-soaked lesions on leaves, turns brown-black. White mold on underside in humid conditions. Very fast spread. Can destroy crop in 72 hours.

EMERGENCY Natural Treatment:
1. Copper hydroxide spray (allowed in organic) — 3–4g/L immediately
2. Remove all infected plants and burn — DO NOT compost
3. Increase plant spacing for air circulation
4. Avoid overhead irrigation — drip only
5. Neem + turmeric spray (10ml neem + 5g turmeric per liter)

Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe species) — Cucurbits, Peas, Vegetables:
SYMPTOMS: White powdery coating on leaves and stems. Appears in dry, warm weather. Reduces photosynthesis.

Natural Treatment:
1. Baking soda spray: 10g sodium bicarbonate + 5ml soap per liter water
2. Milk spray: Dilute fresh milk 1:9 with water — spray weekly
3. Potassium bicarbonate 5g/L (very effective organic fungicide)
4. Remove heavily infected leaves
5. Spray with cow urine 1:10 dilution — excellent anti-fungal

Downy Mildew — Cucurbits, Grapes, Onion:
SYMPTOMS: Yellow patches on leaf top with grey-purple mold on underside.

Natural Treatment:
1. Copper-based spray (copper sulfate + lime = Bordeaux mixture 1%)
2. Reduce irrigation frequency immediately
3. Apply trichoderma viride (biocontrol agent) at root zone
4. Improve field drainage to reduce humidity

Damping Off — Seedlings:
SYMPTOMS: Seeds germinate but seedlings collapse at soil level. Stem becomes water-soaked at base.

Prevention (Beejamrut treatment is key):
1. Treat seeds with Beejamrut before sowing
2. Use well-drained seed beds
3. Trichoderma-enriched compost in nursery beds
4. Avoid overwatering during germination phase

Root Rot — Multiple crops:
SYMPTOMS: Yellowing, wilting despite adequate water. Roots turn brown/black. Foul smell.

Treatment:
1. Drench with Trichoderma harzianum solution (5g/L)
2. Improve drainage immediately (make furrows)
3. Apply wood ash around stem base (raises soil pH slightly)
4. Jeevamrut drench at 3x dose (500L/acre)

Rust Diseases — Wheat, Maize, Beans:
SYMPTOMS: Orange-red pustules on leaves and stems. Spreads rapidly in humid cool conditions.

Natural Treatment:
1. Wettable sulfur 3g/L — most effective organic fungicide for rust
2. Increase air circulation by thinning
3. Apply Panchgavya preventively during cool wet periods
4. Do not re-use infected seeds

BACTERIAL DISEASES

Bacterial Blight — Rice, Cotton:
SYMPTOMS: Water-soaked margins on leaves, turns yellow-brown. Ooze in morning.

Natural Treatment:
1. Copper hydroxide 3g/L spray
2. Remove infected plants
3. Seed treatment with Pseudomonas fluorescens bioagent
4. Avoid nitrogen excess (encourages bacterial growth)

VIRAL DISEASES

Leaf Curl Virus — Tomato, Chilli, Cotton:
SYMPTOMS: Leaves curl upward/downward. Stunted growth. Mottled yellow-green coloring.

Natural Treatment (Viruses cannot be "treated" — only managed):
1. REMOVE AND DESTROY infected plants immediately — they are virus sources
2. Control whitefly vectors — neem oil, yellow sticky traps
3. Plant virus-resistant varieties in next season
4. Maintain weed-free border (weeds harbor whiteflies)
5. Spray cow urine 1:5 to boost plant immunity

SOIL-BORNE DISEASE PREVENTION PROTOCOL

Apply this sequence every season for healthy soil:
1. Pre-sowing: Apply Trichoderma-enriched compost at 250kg/acre
2. Sowing: Treat seeds with Beejamrut
3. 15 DAS: Jeevamrut soil drench (200L/acre)
4. 30 DAS: Repeat Jeevamrut drench
5. Prevention spray: Panchgavya or neem 5ml/L — monthly

Disease-Suppressive Soil Indicators (Healthy Soil = Disease Resistant Crop):
- Earthworm presence: 50+ per square meter = excellent
- Soil crumbles easily without clumping
- Pleasant earthy smell (petrichor)
- Capillary rise visible after watering

Source: ICAR Disease Management Handbook, NCIPM Natural Farming Disease Control Manual, Andhra Pradesh Natural Farming Society (APNFS) Pest & Disease Guide 2022
