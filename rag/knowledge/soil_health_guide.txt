SOIL HEALTH IN NATURAL FARMING — BUILDING LIVING SOIL

UNDERSTANDING SOIL IN ZBNF PHILOSOPHY:

Subhash Palekar's Principle: "Soil is not dead matter — it is a living community of billions of organisms. A single gram of healthy soil contains more living organisms than there are humans on Earth."

The natural farmer's job is not to "fertilize" the crop but to "feed the soil life" — the soil life will then feed the crop.

SOIL HEALTH INDICATORS (Field Assessment):

Physical Indicators:
- Tilth: Good soil crumbles easily in hand — no hard clods
- Color: Dark brown/black soil = high organic matter
- Smell: Pleasant earthen petrichor smell = high microbial activity
- Moisture: Soil remains moist for longer after rain = good water holding capacity
- Roots: Deep, branched roots = good soil structure

Biological Indicators:
- Earthworm count: >10 earthworms per cubic foot = healthy soil
- Fungal threads visible: White fungal mycelium when you dig = mycorrhizal activity
- Decomposing organic matter present
- Birds feeding in field = diverse insect life below

Chemical Indicators (Soil Test):
- pH 6.5–7.5 = ideal for most crops
- Organic Carbon (OC) >0.75% = minimum healthy; >1.5% = excellent
- Low EC (<1 dS/m) = no salt stress
- Balanced NPK + micronutrients

Soil Health Card: Get it done free from nearest KVK every 2 years

BUILDING ORGANIC MATTER IN SOIL:

Method 1 — Mulching (Most Important):
- Cover soil surface with 4–6 inch layer of organic matter
- Options: Dry grass/leaves, crop residue, straw, tree leaves
- Benefits: Conserves moisture, feeds soil microbes, suppresses weeds
- Result: 0.2% OC increase per season with regular mulching

Method 2 — Green Manuring:
- Grow Dhaincha (Sesbania bispinosa) or Sunnhemp before main crop
- Cut and incorporate at flowering stage (4–6 weeks after sowing)
- Adds 40–60 kg N/ha equivalent
- Best green manure crops for India: Dhaincha, Cowpea, Sunhemp, Guar

Method 3 — Compost Application:
- Farm-made compost from crop residue + cow dung + water
- Apply 2–3 tons/acre before sowing
- Never buy chemical/synthetic fertilizers — use only organic compost

Method 4 — Jeevamrut (Liquid Compost):
- Activates soil microbes dramatically
- Start working: Within 72 hours of application
- Long-term: Monthly Jeevamrut increases soil carbon steadily

EARTHWORM FARMING (VERMICOMPOST):
Earthworms are the "soil engineers" — they:
- Create channels for air and water infiltration
- Produce castings with 5x nutrients of regular soil
- Secrete growth hormones that boost root development

Setting Up Vermicompost Pit:
- Size: 6 feet × 3 feet × 2 feet per pit
- Layer: Organic waste (50%) + cow dung (50%) + water
- Earthworms: Eisenia fetida (Red Wigglers) — 1 kg per pit
- Temperature: Must be below 35°C — place in shade
- Harvest: Every 45–60 days (dark, crumbly material at bottom)
- Application: 500 kg vermicompost/acre = equivalent to 100kg urea

SOIL pH CORRECTION:

Acid Soil (pH < 6.5):
Natural Correctors:
- Agricultural lime (limestone powder): 200–300 kg/acre
- Wood ash: 100–200 kg/acre (also adds K and Ca)
- Oyster shell powder: 100 kg/acre
- Apply 4–6 weeks before planting

Alkaline Soil (pH > 7.5) — Common in Rajasthan, North India:
Natural Correctors:
- Gypsum (calcium sulfate): 100–200 kg/acre
- Sulfur (elemental): 20–30 kg/acre
- Compost and organic matter (naturally acidifying)
- Pyrite (iron sulfate): 25 kg/acre for highly alkaline soils

MICRONUTRIENT DEFICIENCY CORRECTION:

Zinc Deficiency (White bud in maize, short internodes in paddy):
- Natural: Zinc solubilizing bacteria (ZSB) applied with Jeevamrut
- Acceptable: Zinc sulfate 20 kg/acre (soil application, once in 3 years)
- Foliar: 0.5% zinc sulfate spray

Iron Deficiency (Interveinal chlorosis — yellow between green veins):
- Natural: Ferrous sulfate 0.5% foliar spray
- Organic chelated iron products
- Improve drainage (waterlogged soils lock iron)

Boron Deficiency (Hollow heart in potato, fruit set failure in vegetables):
- Borax 200g/acre dissolved in water — foliar spray
- Critical for fruit set in cotton, groundnut, sunflower

Manganese Deficiency:
- Manganese sulfate 0.3% foliar spray
- Common in alkaline soils with high pH

SOIL CARBON TRANSFORMATION — THE NF JOURNEY:

Year 1: OC typically 0.3–0.5% (degraded conventional soil)
- Start all NF practices
- Yield may be similar or slightly less than conventional
- DO NOT STOP — soil is transforming

Year 2: OC reaches 0.5–0.8%
- Yield returns to conventional level
- Soil structure visibly improves
- Earthworm activity increases
- Input costs drop 30–40%

Year 3 onwards: OC >1%
- Yield 10–25% ABOVE conventional baseline
- Drought resistance dramatically improved
- Almost no purchased inputs needed
- Soil is alive and self-sustaining

THE FARMER'S MOST IMPORTANT TOOL:
A spade. Dig 1 foot into your field every week. Observe: earthworms, roots, moisture, texture. Your soil is telling you everything you need to know.
