RABI SEASON CROPS — PLANTING GUIDE FOR NATURAL FARMING

RABI SEASON OVERVIEW:
Rabi crops are sown in October–November and harvested in February–March.
Primary states: Punjab, Haryana, UP, MP, Bihar, Rajasthan

KEY RABI CROPS IN NATURAL FARMING:

WHEAT (GEHOON) — Triticum aestivum:
Best Varieties for NF:
- For Punjab/Haryana: HD 3086, HD 2781, HD 2967, WH 711
- For UP/Bihar: GW 496, K 307, Sonalika
- Heritage/Indigenous: Sharbati wheat (MP), Khapli (emmer wheat — Maharashtra, drought resistant)
- Avoid: PBW 343, PBW 502 — heavy input dependent

Planting Calendar:
- Sowing: 1–30 November (timely sown)
- Late sowing: December (HD varieties preferred)
- Harvest: March–April

Seed Rate: 40–45 kg/acre (timely) | 50–55 kg/acre (late sowing)
Seed Treatment: Beejamrut — soak 30 minutes before sowing

Jeevamrut Schedule for Wheat:
- Application 1: 15–20 DAS (crown root initiation)
- Application 2: 40–45 DAS (tillering stage — most critical)
- Application 3: 60–65 DAS (flowering stage)
- Foliar spray: Diluted Jeevamrut (1:10) at 75 DAS (grain filling)

Expected Yield: 16–20 quintals/acre with NF (vs 18–22 conventional)
Note: Yield may be slightly lower initially but stabilizes by Year 3 as soil improves

Wheat Disease Management:
- Loose smut: Beejamrut treatment prevents 80% of infection
- Yellow rust (stripe rust): Wettable sulfur 3g/L spray at early sign
- Powdery mildew: Milk spray (1:9) or baking soda 10g/L

Irrigation for Wheat (Critical Stages):
- Crown root initiation: 20–25 DAS (MOST critical — never skip)
- Tillering: 45 DAS
- Jointing: 60–65 DAS
- Flowering/anthesis: 80–85 DAS
- Dough stage: 100–105 DAS
- Milk stage: 115 DAS (last irrigation)

MUSTARD (SARSON) — Brassica juncea:
Best NF Varieties: Pusa Agrani, Pusa Mahak, RH 30, Bio-902
Planting: 1–20 October | Harvest: February–March

Natural Farming Advantage: Mustard is allelopathic — suppresses weeds naturally
Spacing: 30cm x 10cm (line sowing) | 45cm x 15cm (for small farmers)

Aphid Management (Key Challenge):
- Natural: Imidacloprid is NOT allowed; use neem oil 5ml/L
- Spray timing: When 50+ aphids per plant (ETL)
- Parasitic wasps (Diaeretiella rapae) naturally control aphids — encourage by NOT using chemicals

CHICKPEA (CHANA) — Cicer arietinum:
Varieties: JG 11, JAKI 9218, Pusa 256, KPG 59
Planting: 25 October – 15 November | Harvest: February–March

NF Advantage: N-fixing legume — adds 40–50 kg N/ha to soil
Highly drought-tolerant — suits rain-shadow zones (Rajasthan, Maharashtra plateau)

Boron Requirement: Very sensitive to boron deficiency
Natural Boron Source: Borax 200g/acre dissolved in Jeevamrut

Wilt (Fusarium) Management:
- Most devastating disease of chickpea
- Use wilt-resistant varieties (JG 11 is resistant)
- Trichoderma viride seed treatment
- Beejamrut treatment essential

POTATO (ALOO) — Solanum tuberosum:
Best NF Varieties: Kufri Chandramukhi, Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Bahar, Kufri Sindhuri
Planting: 15 September – 15 November (plains) | Harvest: January–February

Seed Potato Treatment:
- Do NOT use Beejamrut liquid — dip cut tubers in wood ash + neem powder
- Let cut surface dry for 24 hours before planting

NF Soil Requirement: Potatoes need loose, well-drained soil
- Deep plough to 20cm
- Add compost 2 tons/acre before planting
- Ridge formation: 30cm high ridges

Potato Disease Focus:
- Late blight: Major challenge; Copper hydroxide spray preventively before rain
- Common scab: Maintain soil pH below 7.0; add compost to lower pH
- Black scurf: Trichoderma seed treatment

ONION (PYAAZ) — Allium cepa:
NF Varieties: Nasik Red, N 53, Agrifound Light Red, Phule Samarth
Nursery: August–September | Transplanting: October–November | Harvest: March–April

Sulfur Requirement (Critical for onion pungency and yield):
Natural Source: Gypsum (calcium sulfate) 100kg/acre applied at planting

Pink Root Disease: Beejamrut treatment of seedling roots before transplanting
Purple Blotch: Copper-based spray or neem oil 10ml/L at 7-day intervals

GARLIC (LAHSUN) — Allium sativum:
Planting: October | Harvest: March–April
Clove Treatment: Beejamrut + Trichoderma powder mixture

Storage (Post-harvest): Natural farming garlic stores for 8–10 months without chemical treatment because of lower water content

GRAM/LENTIL (MASOOR) — Lens culinaris:
Duration: 100–110 days
NF Advantage: Extreme nitrogen-fixing ability — 50–60 kg N/ha
Post-harvest: Incorporate dry plant matter into soil — releases nitrogen slowly

GENERAL RABI FARMING NOTES:

Timing is Critical:
- Wheat sown after 15 December loses 1 quintal yield per week
- Chickpea sown before October 15: vegetative growth, less yield
- Mustard sown after November 15: escapes peak aphid pressure

Water Management in Rabi:
Rabi crops use 60% less water than Kharif
Golden Rule: "Irrigate at critical stages, never casually"
Water-stress tolerance of rabi crops (from most to least):
Chickpea > Mustard > Barley > Wheat > Potato > Onion

Cold Wave Protection:
- Light irrigation before frost protects crops (latent heat)
- Smoke at dawn in orchards
- Mulching for vegetable nurseries
